## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are basketball players and trainers reviewing shooting form on desktop. They arrive with a clear task: upload an image or short video, inspect the motion, and get actionable analysis without wandering through decorative UI.

### Brand Personality
Focused, cinematic, credible.

The product should feel like a premium basketball analysis tool, not a generic AI dashboard and not a marketing landing page pretending to be software. It should evoke confidence, concentration, and a sense of entering a serious training environment.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark cinematic desktop UI with restrained basketball cover art. Home can feel dramatic and poster-like, but inner pages should become efficient workbenches with cover images reduced to blurred atmospheric backdrops. Motion should be purposeful and signature-driven: one strong fog transition into the work area, then fast and disciplined interactions.

Anti-references:

- generic AI SaaS dashboards
- evenly weighted card grids that make every module feel equally important
- glass-on-glass clutter
- overexplained workspaces with too many labels and repeated helper text
- single-page scrolling that fakes navigation

### Design Principles
1. One page, one job.
2. Home opens the story; inner pages do the work.
3. Cover art supports atmosphere, never information hierarchy.
4. Motion should clarify state changes, not compete with them.
5. The fastest path to upload and analysis must stay obvious in the default desktop window.
